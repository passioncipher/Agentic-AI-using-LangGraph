## Discuss Agentic AI Evolution

Based on the sources, the evolution from Generative AI to Agentic AI represents a fundamental shift from systems that reactively create content to autonomous systems that proactively achieve complex goals. 

The sources illustrate this evolution through a practical scenario: an HR recruiter tasked with hiring a backend engineer. The evolution is broken down into four distinct stages:

**Stage 1: Simple Generative AI (LLM Chatbots)**
Initially, a user could utilise a standard Large Language Model (LLM) chatbot to assist with the hiring process. The human could ask the chatbot to draft a Job Description (JD), suggest platforms for posting the job, formulate interview questions, or write an offer letter. 
*   **Limitations:** This system was entirely **reactive**, meaning the human had to initiate and guide every single step. Furthermore, it lacked memory (context awareness), could only offer highly generic advice rather than company-specific insights, and was incapable of taking real-world actions like actually posting the JD online.

**Stage 2: RAG-Based Chatbots (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**
To solve the issue of generic advice, the chatbot was integrated with the company's internal knowledge base, including past JDs, hiring playbooks, salary bands, and onboarding templates. 
*   **Improvements & Limitations:** This allowed the AI to generate highly specific, tailor-made content that fit the company's DNA. However, it was still a reactive system that relied on human prompting for every step and could not independently execute actions outside of generating text.

**Stage 3: Tool-Augmented Chatbots**
The next evolutionary leap gave the AI the power to take actions by integrating it with external tools and APIs, such as LinkedIn, resume parsers, email servers, and calendar applications. 
*   **Improvements & Limitations:** The AI could now physically post the job to LinkedIn, download and parse candidate resumes, and send out interview emails. Despite this massive improvement in utility, the system was still reactive and lacked the ability to strategise or adapt. The human still had to instruct the AI to check the application numbers or ask it to send the emails. 

**Stage 4: Agentic AI**
The final stage of this evolution is the Agentic AI system, which is **proactive, context-aware, and adaptable**. Instead of micromanaging the AI step-by-step, the human simply provides an end goal: "I want to hire a remote backend engineer".
*   **How it works:** The Agentic AI autonomously plans the entire hiring pipeline. It drafts the JD, posts it, and continuously monitors the application rate. If it notices that application numbers are too low, it **adapts** by independently suggesting a new strategy, such as broadening the JD to include full-stack engineers and boosting the post with ads. It screens resumes, checks the human's calendar to schedule interviews, and automatically triggers IT onboarding once an offer is accepted. 
*   **The Human's Role:** In an Agentic AI system, the human steps back from micromanaging and primarily functions to provide approvals, while the AI handles the heavy lifting. 

**Conclusion**
To summarise the evolution, Generative AI focuses on the **reactive creation of content** across various modalities (text, image, video). Conversely, Agentic AI focuses on **autonomously achieving a goal** through planning and execution. Ultimately, Generative AI serves as a foundational building block for Agentic AI, providing the reasoning and planning capabilities necessary for the agent to behave autonomously.
